The Carbon Cycle

Understanding how carbon moves through our Earth

1. What is the Carbon Cycle?

The carbon cycle describes how carbon atoms move through the Earth's systems — air, water, soil, plants, animals, and rocks. It supports life and helps regulate Earth's temperature.

2. Main Steps in the Carbon Cycle

  • Photosynthesis: Plants take in CO₂ and produce oxygen and food.
  • Respiration: Living things release CO₂ during breathing.
  • Decomposition: Dead organisms break down and return carbon to the soil and atmosphere.
  • Combustion: Burning fossil fuels releases stored carbon.
  • Ocean Absorption: Oceans absorb a large amount of CO₂ from the atmosphere.

3. Why is the Carbon Cycle Important?

Without the carbon cycle, Earth would be uninhabitable. It keeps CO₂ levels balanced. Disrupting this cycle (by pollution, deforestation, etc.) can lead to dangerous climate change.

4. Human Impact on the Carbon Cycle

Humans affect the carbon cycle by burning fossil fuels, cutting trees, and polluting the oceans. These actions increase atmospheric CO₂ and cause global warming.

5. How Can We Help?

  • Use renewable energy sources like solar and wind.
  • Plant trees and conserve forests.
  • Recycle and reduce waste.
  • Use eco-friendly transport — walk, cycle, or take public transit.
